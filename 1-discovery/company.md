# Company Research — capq.ai (CQ)

> Researched 2026-05-20 (revised 2026-05-21). Sources: homepage, `/insights`, `/handbook`. `/about` exists but could not be fetched by tooling (likely bot-blocked) — see "Open follow-ups" at bottom.

## Product

**What it does.** CQ is an AI-powered fundraising platform for emerging fund managers raising capital across alternatives (private equity, venture, hedge funds, secondaries). It covers an 8-stage lifecycle — LP discovery → AI matching → outreach automation → secure data room → NDA signing → LP portal → analytics → ongoing updates — positioning itself as "the only platform covering all 8 stages" versus competitors that cover 2–3.

**Key product surface:**

- **LP Database:** 150K+ LP profiles with verified contacts and mandate matching.
- **AI Matching:** ranks LPs by fit for a given fund thesis.
- **AI Outreach:** automated cold email sequences.
- **AI Data Room:** 24/7 AI concierge inside a secure room; auto-NDA signing.
- **LP Portal + Analytics:** deal exploration, commitment tracking, update distribution.

### Buying triggers (when they arrive on capq.ai)

1. **Just incorporated the fund** — building first LP list from scratch.
2. **First close coming up** — chaos managing LP comms in spreadsheets + Gmail.
3. **Fund II raise underway** — LP list is stale; yes-to-no ratio worsening.
4. **Peer recommendation** — heard CQ name dropped at a conference / on a Substack / on a podcast.
5. **Replatforming** — outgrowing Mailchimp + DocSend + Notion stitched together.

### Decision-making process

- **Self-serve buyer.** GP discovers, signs up free, evaluates 1–2 weeks, then upgrades. No procurement, no IT, no committee — minimal legal review (DPA only).
- **Time-to-decide:** typically 1–4 weeks.
- **Adjacent stakeholders:** fund admin partner (recommends tools), legal counsel (signs DPA), peer GPs (word-of-mouth validation). None block — they advise.
- **Spend authority:** the GP signs off without partnership approval up to ~$10K/year.

### Where the ICP lives online

- **LinkedIn** — default professional surface, especially for emerging GPs who post for credibility / personal-brand.
- **Substacks** — Carta's "On Carta", Lerer / VC Hour / "Capital Allocators" orbit, AltsSociety, individual emerging-GP newsletters.
- **Podcasts** — *The Twenty Minute VC* (VC slice), *Capital Allocators* (PE/HF), *Acquired*, *Founders Podcast*.
- **Forums / Reddit** — r/venturecapital, r/privateequity. Heavily lurked, less posted.
- **Conferences** — SuperReturn (PE/HF), ACG (corporate dev / private capital), Bloomberg / Goldman alts summits.
- **Twitter/X** — smaller but active capital-markets corner.

### Secondary segment — Established small GPs frustrated with stitched-together tools

- $250M–$1B funds running Affinity + DocSend + Mailchimp + spreadsheets.
- Buyer: IR Manager or Operations Partner (not the GP).
- Trigger: a tool consolidation initiative, or a slower Fund III raise prompting a process rethink.

### Tertiary segment — Family offices spinning out into formal GPs

- Family office team raising external capital for the first time.
- Buyer: head of the family office or their CIO.
- Trigger: regulatory shift (single-LP exempt → multi-LP regulated).

### Anti-ICP (who CQ is NOT for)

- **Mega-funds (Fund IV+, $1B+ AUM)** — have dedicated IR teams + enterprise platforms (DealCloud, Carta Studio).
- **Non-fund vehicles** — rolling funds, SPV-only operators. Workflow doesn't fit.
- **Relationship-first VCs** — partnerships preferring Affinity-style CRMs over data-first matching.

## Voice & positioning

**Voice.** Founder-led and conversational on the homepage ("Built by an asset manager"). Authoritative and systematic in the handbook. Generic and unbylined in `/insights`. Each surface serves a different job — these aren't in conflict, just unintegrated.

**Differentiators claimed.**

- "Only platform covering all 8 stages" of the fundraising lifecycle (vs. competitors covering 2–3).
- Quantified ROI claims: "10 hrs saved weekly," "2x faster closes."
- Founder-operator credibility: "$455M+ invested by our team," "50+ deals closed by our team."
- Network advantage: "150K+ LP profiles" with mandate matching.

**Differentiators NOT yet defended in marketing:**

- No named competitive comparison (no "vs. Carta", "vs. Affinity", "vs. DealCloud").
- No external customer logos, testimonials, or case studies — all proof points are internal team metrics.
- No demo video or product walkthrough; visitors must book a demo to see the product.
- No security/compliance certifications mentioned despite handling LP data (SOC 2, ISO).
