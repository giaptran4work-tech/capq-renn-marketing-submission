# Company Research — capq.ai (CQ)

> Researched 2026-05-20. Sources: homepage, `/insights`, `/handbook`, attempted `/about` (404).

## Product

**What it does.** CQ is an AI-powered fundraising platform for emerging fund managers raising capital across alternatives (private equity, venture, hedge funds, secondaries). It covers an 8-stage lifecycle — LP discovery → AI matching → outreach automation → secure data room → NDA signing → LP portal → analytics → ongoing updates — positioning itself as "the only platform covering all 8 stages" versus competitors that cover 2–3.

**Key product surface:**

- **LP Database:** 150K+ LP profiles with verified contacts and mandate matching.
- **AI Matching:** ranks LPs by fit for a given fund thesis.
- **AI Outreach:** automated cold email sequences.
- **AI Data Room:** 24/7 AI concierge inside a secure room; auto-NDA signing.
- **LP Portal + Analytics:** deal exploration, commitment tracking, update distribution.

## Target audience / ICP

- **Industry:** Alternative asset management — private equity, venture capital, hedge funds, secondaries.
- **Role of buyer:** Emerging fund managers / GPs raising inaugural or sub-Fund-III vehicles. Likely founder-operators who are also doing the fundraising themselves (vs. having dedicated IR teams).
- **Company size:** Small, lean shops — the handbook talks about "fund one" workflows, regulatory framing for solo GPs, and the platform self-service flow (`start free`) targets buyers who can't afford an enterprise sales cycle.
- **Where they live online:** LinkedIn (institutional finance default), niche alts-focused Substacks, fund-formation legal blogs, conferences (SuperReturn, ACG, etc.). Capital-markets Twitter/X for some segments.

## Current marketing surface

- **Top nav:** Log in, Get started free (minimal — funnels to signup or auth).
- **Footer (Product):** Overview, Database, Matching, Outreach, Data Room, NDA Signing, Portal, Analytics, Updates, Hub, Collaboration, Pricing.
- **Footer (Resources):** Handbook, Insights, Glossary, Database.
- **Footer (Company):** About Us, Contact.
- **`/about` returns 404** — broken or missing page (credibility hit + SEO problem).

**Content / blog.**

- `/insights`: ~67 articles. **All visible posts dated "Dec 2025"** — suggests a single bulk-publish event (probably AI-generated content drop) rather than ongoing cadence.
- Format: long-form (10–20 min), SEO-optimized titles ("Best Fund Management Software", "Top PE Firms"), some listicles.
- **Anonymous bylines** across all posts — no individual contributor attribution.
- No comments, share counts, or engagement signals embedded.
- Internal linking is moderate (to product pages + category filters) but not densely interconnected.

**Gated assets.**

- **The Fund One Handbook** (`/handbook`): 12-chapter comprehensive guide covering thesis, sourcing, fund structuring, SEC compliance, LP data rooms, segmentation, fundraising workflows, closes, reporting, secondaries. Promises "50+ templates."
- **Ungated** — no email wall. Serves as a lead magnet via in-page CTAs (`Start reading`, `Get started free`, `Book a strategy call`).

**SEO posture.** Title-tag and URL strategy is keyword-first. Content is substantive enough to rank, but the bulk-publish dating signal (every post = "Dec 2025") will dilute freshness signals over time.

**Paid presence.** Not investigated in detail in this pass; the homepage doesn't lead with retargeting/social pixel evidence, but a more rigorous SEO+ads audit belongs in Task 2.

**Social / community.** LinkedIn and X linked in footer only. No on-site community (no comments, no newsletter signup visible).

## Voice & positioning

**Voice.** Founder-led and conversational on the homepage ("Built by an asset manager"), authoritative-but-systematic in the handbook, **anonymous and generic in Insights**. This three-way inconsistency is a positioning problem: the founder-led credibility play on the homepage is undermined by faceless content marketing.

**Differentiators claimed.**

- "Only platform covering all 8 stages" of the fundraising lifecycle (vs. competitors covering 2–3).
- Quantified ROI claims: "10 hrs saved weekly," "2x faster closes."
- Founder-operator credibility: "$455M+ invested by our team," "50+ deals closed by our team."
- Network advantage: "150K+ LP profiles" with mandate matching.

**Differentiators NOT yet defended in marketing:**

- No named competitive comparison (no "vs. Carta", "vs. Affinity", "vs. DealCloud").
- No external customer logos, testimonials, or case studies — all proof points are internal team metrics.
- No demo video or product walkthrough; visitors must book a demo to see the product.
- No security/compliance certifications mentioned despite handling LP data (SOC 2, ISO).
